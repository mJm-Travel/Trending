Introducing Zoho CRM Integration

Posted on August 13, 2025 by Vigneshwaran SE
2 Mins Read

If you manage multiple business locations using Zoho CRM, integrating with Zoho Publish makes it easier to track their performance, monitor online reputation, and stay updated on business information changes.

This integration syncs your Google Business Profile data — including store details, reviews, and ratings — directly into Zoho CRM. It helps you centralize key business insights, compare performance across locations, and keep every team on the same page.

What does this integration offer?  

Connecting Zoho Publish with Zoho CRM gives you a smarter way to oversee businesses. This integration helps you bring everything into one place. Instead of switching between tools or digging into separate dashboards, your teams can now work with live, actionable data.

Sync all your business info

Business information, including store hours, contact details, and descriptions from your Google Business Profiles, is synced automatically into your CRM. No manual updates. No inconsistencies. Just up-to-date information where your teams need it.

Track performance using the Publish Score 

Every business location gets a Publish Score, a simple 0 to 100 metric calculated using review count, response time, response rate, and average rating. It helps you compare how different locations are performing at a glance.

Understand customer sentiment at a glance 

Get a clear view of how customers feel about each location. With reviews insights synced into CRM, spotting satisfaction dips or emerging concerns becomes easier than ever.

Make faster, smarter decisions 

When crucial review data comes together in your CRM, you gain a clearer picture of what’s working and what’s not. Your team can prioritize locations that need attention and take action before issues grow.

Final thoughts  

Your CRM just got a lot more powerful. By connecting Zoho Publish with Zoho CRM, you're not just managing sales; you're tracking reputation, customer satisfaction, and location-level performance.
